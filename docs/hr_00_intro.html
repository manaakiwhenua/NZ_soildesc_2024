13.1 Composition

Each soil horizon comprises the following major components:

  • Mineral fraction
    • fine earth (mineral particles < 2mm)
    • rock fragments (mineral particles > 2mm)
    • concentrations (precipitates of any size)
  • Biological fraction
    • Fine organic matter
    • Coarse organic matter (timber, plant roots)
  • Liquid fraction (moisture content)
  • Voids

These fractions can be communicated as percentage by volume, adding to 100%. The fine-earth fraction also has its own compositional data based on finer divisions of the <2 mm fraction (see Chapter 16). Volumetric water content is best determined in the laboratory, and so is not described further in this section.

13.7 Origins

13.7.1 Parent material origin

Parent material origin, or mode of emplacement, describes how soil parent materials have arrived at the point of observation. Multiple modes may have operated in different parts of the soil profile.

Record one parent material origin code per soil horizon, using the codes in Table 24.5. At the horizon level, the An Anthropic class can be subdivided by origin using the codes in Table 13.1 (e.g. Ann for a fill pad in a residential housing development).

Parent material origin is also summarised across the top 100 cm of the soil profile as part of defining the NZSC Soil Family (Section 24.1.5).
