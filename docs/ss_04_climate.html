6.1.1 Global-Regional

The Köppen-Geiger climate classification (Köppen 1936) is the most well-known and accepted global system, and has been periodically updated (e.g. (Kottek et al. 2006; Beck et al. 2018, 2023)). Current maps and projections under various climate change scenarios are available from https://www.gloh2o.org/koppen/.

In and around New Zealand, the Köppen regions that currently occur are listed in Table 6.1.

Since most work programs will take place within one zone, regional climate rarely needs to be noted in the field.

Where a study area appears to deviate from a regional map, the extent and nature of the deviation should be discussed in project reporting.

6.1.2 Local

Various classifications specific to New Zealand have been produced (Robertson 1956; Garr and Fitzharris 1991; Sturman, McGowan, and Spronken-Smith 1999).

NIWA have more recently published a useful summary classification at https://niwa.co.nz/climate-and-weather/overview-new-zealands-climate, although it is not accompanied by spatial data. The classification is briefly reproduced in Table 6.2 below.

  • summer/winter ratio (Dec+Jan+Feb)/(June+Jul+Aug)

6.1.2.2 Temperature regime

  • mean annual, etc

6.1.2.3 Insolation regime

6.2 Paleoclimate

Soil formation, as a long-term phenomenon, has in many cases a stronger connection to past climates than the present one. Understanding of New Zealand paleoclimates is relatively well-developed, partly due to the intensive study of volcanic and seismic activity on the North Island (Williams et al. 2015). …

6.3 Weather

Contemporary weather conditions have not necessarily had a strong influence on soil formation, except perhaps in young and/or rapidly developing soils. These parameters are more relevant for agricultural suitability assessment and for interpreting soil descriptions.

6.3.1 Current Weather

Record the weather at the time of observation using the codes in Table 6.3, plus temperature in degrees Celsius. These are not always essential, but can affect colour recording and some field tests.

e.g. SU 23 for a perfect day in the field.
